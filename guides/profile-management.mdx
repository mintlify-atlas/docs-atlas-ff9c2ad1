Your profile page is the central hub for managing your activity, assets, and settings on the AgrospAI Portal.

## Accessing Your Profile

<Steps>
<Step title="Connect your wallet">
  Make sure your [wallet is connected](/guides/wallet-setup) to the portal.
</Step>

<Step title="Navigate to your profile">
  Click on your wallet address in the top right corner of the portal, then select **Profile** from the dropdown menu.

  Alternatively, you can directly access your profile by clicking your avatar or address.
</Step>
</Steps>

## ENS Integration

The portal integrates with Ethereum Name Service (ENS) for enhanced identity.

### What is ENS?

ENS allows you to register a human-readable name (like `yourname.eth`) instead of using your long wallet address.

### Benefits of ENS

<Check>
  - Replace your address with a memorable name
  - Set a custom avatar
  - Add verified credentials
  - Build reputation and trust
  - Easier for others to find and recognize you
</Check>

### Setting Up ENS

<Steps>
<Step title="Register an ENS name">
  Visit [app.ens.domains](https://app.ens.domains) and register a `.eth` name:

  1. Search for available names
  2. Select your desired name
  3. Complete registration (requires ETH on Ethereum mainnet)
  4. Set your wallet as the primary address
</Step>

<Step title="Configure your ENS profile">
  On the ENS app:

  - **Set Primary Name**: Make this ENS name your primary identity
  - **Add Avatar**: Upload an image or NFT as your avatar
  - **Add Records**: Include website, Twitter, email, etc.
  - **Set Reverse Record**: Link your address to your ENS name
</Step>

<Step title="View on AgrospAI Portal">
  Once configured, the portal automatically:
  - Displays your ENS name instead of your address
  - Shows your custom avatar
  - Includes ENS records in your profile
  - Makes you more discoverable

  <Note>
    ENS changes may take a few minutes to appear on the portal. Refresh the page if needed.
  </Note>
</Step>
</Steps>

### Verifiable Credentials

The portal supports verifiable credentials linked to your ENS:

- Display verified credentials on your profile
- Build trust with data consumers
- Show organizational affiliations
- Prove identity or certifications

<Info>
  Verifiable credentials are cryptographically signed attestations about your identity or qualifications.
</Info>

## Managing Published Assets

View and manage all assets you've published.

### Published Assets List

<Steps>
<Step title="Navigate to Published Assets">
  On your profile, go to the **History** tab and select **Published**.
</Step>

<Step title="View your assets">
  See all your published datasets and algorithms:

  - Asset name and type
  - Publication date
  - Current price
  - Number of sales/downloads
  - Current status (active, paused, etc.)
  - Revenue earned
</Step>

<Step title="Edit an asset">
  Click on any asset to view details, then click **Edit**:

  You can modify:
  - Name and description
  - Tags and categories
  - Price (but not pricing type)
  - Timeout duration
  - Access policies (allow/deny lists)
  - Compute settings

  <Warning>
    Some fields cannot be changed after publishing (asset type, file URLs, NFT details).
  </Warning>
</Step>

<Step title="Monitor performance">
  For each asset, track:
  - Total downloads or compute jobs
  - Revenue generated
  - Recent activity
  - User feedback (if available)
</Step>
</Steps>

### Asset Management Actions

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Edit Metadata">
    Update descriptive information:
    - Change title or description
    - Add or remove tags
    - Update documentation links
    - Modify sample data

    Click **Edit** on the asset, make changes, and confirm the transaction.
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Update Pricing">
    Adjust pricing:
    - Change the price amount (up or down)
    - Note: Cannot switch between free and paid
    - Transaction required to update on-chain

    Price updates take effect immediately after confirmation.
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Manage Access">
    Control who can access your asset:
    - Add addresses to allow list (whitelist)
    - Add addresses to deny list (blacklist)
    - Remove addresses from lists
    - Set or modify timeout duration

    Changes require a transaction to update on-chain.
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Configure Compute">
    For compute-enabled datasets:
    - Add or remove trusted algorithms
    - Add or remove trusted publishers
    - Enable/disable raw algorithms
    - Enable/disable network access

    <Warning>
      Be cautious when enabling raw algorithms or network access - these can be security risks.
    </Warning>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Tracking Downloads

View all data assets you've purchased and downloaded.

<Steps>
<Step title="Navigate to Downloads">
  On your profile, go to **History** → **Downloads**.
</Step>

<Step title="View download history">
  See all assets you've purchased:

  - Asset name and publisher
  - Purchase date and transaction hash
  - Price paid
  - Access expiration (if applicable)
  - Download button
</Step>

<Step title="Download again">
  For assets you own:
  - Click the asset name to go to its page
  - Or use the download button directly from your history
  - No additional payment required (if access is still valid)

  <Note>
    If access has expired, you'll need to purchase again.
  </Note>
</Step>
</Steps>

## Managing Compute Jobs

Track all your compute-to-data jobs.

<Steps>
<Step title="Navigate to Compute Jobs">
  On your profile, go to **History** → **Compute Jobs**.
</Step>

<Step title="View job history">
  See all compute jobs you've run:

  - Job ID and status
  - Dataset and algorithm used
  - Start time and duration
  - Network and provider
  - Results availability
</Step>

<Step title="View job details">
  Click on any job to see:
  - Complete job configuration
  - All parameters used
  - Detailed status and logs
  - Transaction information
  - Error messages (if failed)
</Step>

<Step title="Download results">
  For completed jobs:
  - Click **Download Results**
  - Save output files locally
  - Review algorithm logs

  <Info>
    Results are typically available for 30 days after job completion.
  </Info>
</Step>
</Steps>

### Job Status Overview

Your compute jobs history shows:

| Status | Meaning | Actions Available |
|--------|---------|------------------|
| Pending | Waiting to start | Monitor, cancel (if supported) |
| Running | Currently executing | Monitor progress |
| Completed | Successfully finished | Download results |
| Failed | Error occurred | View error logs, retry |

## Consent Management

For advanced compute-to-data scenarios involving consent requests.

### What are Consents?

Consents allow:
- Algorithm publishers to request permission to use specific datasets
- Dataset owners to grant/deny access to their algorithms
- Audit trail of data usage permissions

### Managing Consent Requests

<Steps>
<Step title="View consent requests">
  On your profile, go to **History** → **Consents**.

  You'll see:
  - **Incoming Requests**: Others requesting to use your algorithms
  - **Outgoing Requests**: Your requests to use others' datasets
</Step>

<Step title="Review incoming requests">
  For each incoming consent request:

  - Who is requesting access
  - Which dataset they want to use
  - Reason for the request
  - Proposed usage details
</Step>

<Step title="Grant or deny consent">
  For incoming requests:

  1. Click **Inspect** to review details
  2. Read the request reason and proposed usage
  3. Click **Approve** or **Deny**
  4. Optionally add a response message
  5. Confirm the action

  <Note>
    You can revoke granted consents at any time.
  </Note>
</Step>

<Step title="Monitor your requests">
  For outgoing requests:

  - See status (pending, approved, denied)
  - View responses from dataset owners
  - Track which consents are active
  - Delete requests if no longer needed
</Step>
</Steps>

### Consent Petition

To request consent to use a dataset:

1. Navigate to the dataset page
2. Look for **Request Consent** button (if available)
3. Fill out the petition form:
   - Select your algorithm
   - Explain your intended use
   - Provide justification
4. Submit the request
5. Wait for the dataset owner to respond

## Automation Wallet

The portal supports automation wallets for scheduled and automated tasks.

### What is an Automation Wallet?

An automation wallet is a separate wallet you control that can:
- Execute transactions on your behalf
- Run scheduled compute jobs
- Automate data workflows
- Operate without manual intervention

### Setting Up Automation

<Steps>
<Step title="Access automation settings">
  In the portal settings or profile, look for **Automation** options.
</Step>

<Step title="Import automation wallet">
  1. Create or import a wallet for automation
  2. Keep the private key secure
  3. Fund the wallet with tokens for gas and operations
  4. Link it to your main account

  <Warning>
    The automation wallet has significant permissions. Only use it in secure environments.
  </Warning>
</Step>

<Step title="Configure automation rules">
  Set up automated tasks:
  - Scheduled compute jobs
  - Automatic data purchases
  - Recurring operations
</Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
  Your profile shows both your main wallet activity and automation wallet activity.
</Info>

## Export Your Data

Download your activity data for records or analysis.

### Exportable Data

- Transaction history
- Published assets list
- Purchase history
- Compute job records
- Revenue reports
- Consent logs

### How to Export

1. Navigate to **Settings** → **Export Data**
2. Select data types to export
3. Choose format (CSV, JSON)
4. Click **Export**
5. Download the generated file

<Note>
  Data export is useful for accounting, reporting, or backup purposes.
</Note>

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
<Accordion title="Profile not loading">
  - Verify your wallet is connected
  - Check you're on a supported network
  - Try refreshing the page
  - Clear browser cache if issues persist
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Statistics not updating">
  - Data may take a few minutes to sync from the blockchain
  - Refresh the page
  - Check that transactions were confirmed
  - Verify you're viewing the correct wallet address
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Cannot edit published asset">
  - Only the publishing wallet can edit assets
  - Ensure you're connected with the correct wallet
  - Some fields are immutable after publishing
  - Check for any error messages
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="ENS name not showing">
  - Verify ENS is correctly configured on Ethereum mainnet
  - Set reverse record on ENS app
  - Wait a few minutes for changes to propagate
  - Refresh the portal page
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Missing downloads or compute jobs">
  - Check you're connected with the correct wallet
  - Verify transactions were successful
  - Look in the correct network/chain filter
  - Some old jobs may have been archived
</Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
